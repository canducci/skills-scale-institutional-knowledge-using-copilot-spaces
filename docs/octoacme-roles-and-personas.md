# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead oversees the test strategy and quality assurance practices across the project. They ensure that defects are identified early, tracked to resolution, and that the team maintains consistent quality standards throughout delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Define and own the overall test strategy (manual and automated)
- Coordinate QA activities across sprints and releases
- Track defects and ensure timely resolution
- Represent quality concerns in planning and release meetings
- Define and validate acceptance criteria with Product Managers

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Establish repeatable, scalable test coverage
- Promote a quality-first culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and release readiness reviews
- Defect triage meetings with Developers
- Test coverage and quality metrics reports

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Collaborates on test coverage strategies, reviews automated test implementations, and coordinates on defect reproduction and fixes
- **Product Managers:** Works together to define acceptance criteria and validates that delivered features meet user expectations
- **Project Managers:** Provides quality status updates and flags risks that may impact release timelines

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for crafting user-centered experiences. They translate product requirements into intuitive workflows, wireframes, and prototypes, ensuring usability standards are upheld throughout development.

### Responsibilities
- Design user-facing workflows, wireframes, and mockups
- Conduct or coordinate usability testing and research
- Maintain and evolve the product's design system and standards
- Collaborate on feature specifications to ensure design feasibility

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce friction in user flows and minimize support burden
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs
- Collaboration sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test findings and design iteration notes

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Translates product requirements into design concepts and provides feedback on feasibility and user impact
- **Developers:** Reviews implementation for design fidelity, clarifies interaction behaviors, and iterates based on technical constraints
- **Customer Success Manager:** Gathers end-user feedback to inform design improvements and validate workflows

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage the infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and operational reliability of systems. They bridge development and operations to accelerate delivery while maintaining system stability and security.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain infrastructure-as-code and cloud environments
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Monitor system performance, availability, and security posture
- Manage environment provisioning for development, staging, and production

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Maximize system uptime and observability
- Reduce manual operational toil through automation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and deployment coordination sessions
- Incident postmortems and system health reports
- Infrastructure change documentation and runbooks

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers:** Collaborates on pipeline integration, environment requirements, and deployment workflows
- **Product Managers:** Coordinates during release planning to align deployment windows with feature readiness
- **QA Lead:** Ensures test environments are stable, reproducible, and representative of production conditions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather, clarify, and document requirements, ensuring that features are solving real user problems aligned with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business and functional requirements
- Analyze workflows and identify inefficiencies or gaps
- Translate stakeholder needs into actionable user stories or specifications
- Validate that delivered features address the identified business needs

### Goals
- Ensure features deliver measurable business value
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements before development begins
- Facilitate shared understanding between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story refinement sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Requirements documentation and traceability matrices

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Collaborates on backlog refinement and requirements prioritization to ensure business alignment
- **Developers:** Provides detailed context on requirements and clarifies edge cases during implementation
- **QA Lead:** Supports test case design by providing requirements context and acceptance scenarios

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers act as the voice of the customer within the project team. They communicate customer feedback, champion adoption, and ensure that the product delivers ongoing value to end users.

### Responsibilities
- Gather, synthesize, and communicate customer feedback to the product team
- Monitor customer adoption metrics and identify friction points
- Represent customer perspectives in roadmap and prioritization discussions
- Coordinate with support teams to surface recurring issues and trends

### Goals
- Drive customer adoption and long-term satisfaction
- Ensure product improvements reflect real customer pain points
- Close the feedback loop between customers and the development team

### Typical Communication
- Regular feedback summaries and customer health reports
- Roadmap and prioritization meetings with Product Managers
- Coordination with support and QA for issue validation

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers:** Partners to prioritize feedback and ensure the roadmap reflects customer needs and satisfaction drivers
- **Developers:** Provides context on end-user workflows and expectations to inform implementation decisions
- **QA Lead:** Collaborates on validation scenarios derived from real customer use cases and reported issues
